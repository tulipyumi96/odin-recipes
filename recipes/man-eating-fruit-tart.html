Man Eating Fruit Tart

A savory tart with a spicy salt and pepper kick. The pastry is made from tree fruit skins and slime is used instead of gelatin.

Ingredients (for 3-4 servings)

  • Broth from Scorpion soup.....200ml
  • Slime gelatin.....80g
  • Baraselia.....3 medium sized
  • Meeroak.....5-6 medium sized
  • Betan.....3 medium sized
  • Salt.....pinch
  • Pepper.....pinch

Directions

  1. Lightly steam the Meeroak fruit.
  2. Cut around the stem of the Meeroak fruit and pull out the seeds.
  3. Carefully peel the skin off and beat it until soft.
  4. Line your pan with the skin. The skin is to keep the tart mixture from burning.
  5. In another bowl, grind up the fruit and slowly add the Slime and Scorpion broth until the mixture is thick and smooth.
  6. Lightly add salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Cut some of the other tree fruits into bite sized chunks but leave a handful for later.
  8. Throw the fruit chunks into the mixture.
  9. Lightly mix and then pour the mixture into the skin lined pan.
  10. Cook under medium heat.
  11. Once the surface starts to bubble, add the rest of the fruits on top.
  12. Let it cook for a few more minutes and then finish off with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  13. Let the tart sit and cool for a few minutes so the slime hardens.
  14. Enjoy!
